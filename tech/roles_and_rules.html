tech:roles_and_rules

This is the overarching document for backstage roles, responsibilities and rules with regard to technical backstage jobs in the New Theatre.

1. Roles

  1. The Technical Director (TD) is responsible for all technical aspects of the production. They are to liaise with the director, producer, lighting and sound designers before the get-in to organise the technical aspects of the get-in. Any special requirements and major issues should be brought to the attention of the Company Technical Director by the show's TD. The TD is to supervise the rigging of lights and any modifications to the theatre's default setup. The TD is to supervise plotting and to create the cue script. The TD is to run the show, give LX and sound cues and be the contact point with FoH. They are to write up the show report sheet and report to the CTD any major issues. It is the responsibility of the TD to ensure the rigging of electrics for a show adheres to the theatre's health and safety policy, their training will be inline with this. All safety questions related to electrics must be addressed to the Technical Manager (TM). They are responsible for technical parts of the get-out such as derigging and the tidy of tech areas.
  2. The lighting and sound Operators (ops) are the responsibility of the TD. They should attend at least one run through before the get-in to be familiar with the show. For performances they will help prepare the venue and assist the TD with pre-show checks. During the show they operate sound, lighting and/video. After the house is clear they will assist the TD with powering down and other jobs. At all times operators should be given the opportunity to learn new skills, for example during down time they could be taught more advanced functions on the lighting desk. During the get out they assist derigging and tidying.
  3. The Lighting Designer (LD) works with the director, producer and set designer to create the look onstage. Prior to the get-in they are to attend rehearsals, liaise with the TD informing them of special requirements and produce a lighting plot. They are to attend the rigging session and run the focus session. The LD will run the the plotting session as the programmer or working with a programmer (could be TD or an operator) while the TD notes the cue points. During tech and dress runs they are to sit in the audience and take notes on the lighting. Changes to the plot can then happen after the rehearsal. After the show opens the LD's responsibility ends, they can however tweak the plot between shows if it is deemed necessary. During the get out the LD will assist the TD in derigging and tidying of tech areas such as filing the colour away.
  4. The Sound Designer (SD) works with the director and producer to create the soundscape for the show. The position is very flexible, it may encompass working with a composer or simply selecting sound effects. They are to attend rehearsals and to have sourced music and effects prior to the get-in. The SD is responsible for creating the mix and/or cues for the show. They should work with the TD to note the cue points. During tech and dress runs they are to sit in the audience and take notes on the sound. During the get out the SD will assist the TD in derigging and tidying of tech areas such as coiling and storing XLR cables.
  5. The Stage Manager (SM) holds ultimate responsibility for the set and staging of a show. They are to coordinate the construction of the set during the get-in period, in accordance with the needs for the set designer, production team and technical team and should delegate tasks amongst cast and crew members accordingly. After construction they are responsible for the maintenance of the set, repairing any damage and ensuring that the stage is fully prepared for the performance including the maintenance and availability of any props needed. During the show the SM is responsible for the smooth running of the backstage area, ensuring that props are correctly placed, actors correctly positioned and cued and that any problems occurring during a performance are dealt with minimal disruption to the show. To this extent they should coordinate with the director to suit their needs and requirements. In addition the SM should be in communication with the TD, assisting them with cueing the show and passing other relevant information. Furthermore they should annotate and maintain a stage managers script and should be familiar with the show by the time of its run by attending rehearsals and at least one full run prior to the tech rehearsal. They must maintain a safe working environment on stage clearing hazards as they are seen, such as broken glass, and should work to ensure all tasks are performed safely by their team.
  6. Assistant Stage Manager (ASMs) primary role is preparing the set and items used in the production both before, after and during a performance as required. They should ensure all props are positioned correctly. A Stage Hand should also be available during a get in period and have a basic knowledge that allows them to assist in the set construction process and to later undertake any necessary repairs to the set. They should have a level of familiarity with the show to allow for on the night improvisation when problems arise, to this extent they should participate in both the tech and dress rehearsals and have seen one full run of the show prior to this. They should be backstage and maintain access to their own, or the SM’s, script to cue actors where necessary and be aware of prop placement. They should be prepared for further tasks to arise according to the delegation of the SM.

On a show night the director and producer should not be performing technical tasks. The stage is the stage manager's and the tech is the TD's.

2. Rules

2.1 Cleaning and tidying

  1. By the first night backstage areas (wings, tech office and store, corridors) should be in a tidy state. The CTD and TM will assess this.
  2. Before the first night the show must clean and tidy the auditorium as this is the mess from the get in. After this point it is the job of FoH.
  3. The TD of the production is responsible for ensuring all fire routes in the auditorium are clear.
  4. The SM is responsible for ensuring all backstage fire routes are clear.
  5. Points 2 and 3 form part of the show report form and must be checked before the house is opened.
  6. The TD is to ensure the tech box is in a tidy state before the shows, as it is hard to op a show in a messy environment.
  7. The TD is to ensure the tech box and store are tidied during the get out. This will be checked by a committee member.

2.2 Rigging Safety

  1. Lone working is not permitted in any case.
  2. Persons rigging and/or working at height must be trained, competent and have their record signed.
  3. Each show's TD will supervise rigging and have suitable training to do so. If for any reason the TD cannot do this a trained member must do so in their place.
  4. The TM will inspect rigging before the first performance and must declare it safe for the house to open.

2.3 Electrical Safety

  1. All persons installing temporary electrics (such as rigging lights) must be trained, competent and have their training record signed.
  2. As with rigging the TD will supervise installation of electrics and be trained to do so.
  3. The TM will inspect installed electrics before the first performance and must declare them safe for the house to open.
  4. If at any point the safety of the electrics are in question, such as repeated RCD trips, the TD must power down and stop all use of stage and tech box power until the TM can declare the system safe.
  5. Investigation of faults must be carried out by a competent person and due care must be exercised, such as disconnecting the supply before changing a lamp.

2.4 Use of Effects

  1. Use of smoke, haze or strobes must be cleared with the TM.
  2. For a show the TD must check with Front of House, in person or over the intercom, that the fire alarm is isolated before deploying any smoke. During rehearsals the TD should ask a member of committee to isolate the fire alarm. If a member of committee is not involved with the show then the TD and theatre manager should agree on a procedure for isolating the fire alarm
  3. The fire alarm must be de-isolated as soon as possible after a rehearsal/show.
  4. Signs in the foyer are to be displayed indicating the nature of effects used. FoH should be aware how the effects are used so they can inform patrons with concerns.

2.5 Emergency Procedure

NNT evacuation procedure not yet finalised. This document, while not intended to be a copy of the evacuation procedure, should carry relevant information pertaining to the technical staff.

  1. An evacuation starts with a FoH member announcing centre stage: 'Ladies and Gentlemen this performance is to be suspended, please leave the auditorium via your nearest exit.' The sound system should not be used for this task. FoH members will direct the audience to the muster point and maintain calm.
  2. The word 'fire' or the nature of the incident should not be used when communicating to the audience.
  3. Immediately the LX op will turn on all the working lights, followed by turning off all stage lights. The sound op will turn off the amps. The TD will supervise these activities.
  4. If it is safe to do so the TD will power down the dimming and stage power.
  5. The TD will stay with the cast and crew, they will liaise with FoH.
  6. A FoH member will clear all rooms in the building and report to University Security.
  7. When it is safe to do so cast and crew will re-enter, followed by the audience and the show will resume from where it left off.

3. Logistics Meeting

  1. The logistics meeting is held roughly two weeks prior to a show's get-in. It is called by the Theatre Manager.
  2. Attending this meeting will be
    1. The Director and Producer of the show
    2. The show's Technical Director and designers
    3. The Theatre Manager
    4. Either the Company Techincal Director or Technical Manager
    5. Either Workshop Manager
    6. The Company Stage Manager
  3. A committee member will take minutes
  4. The aim of the Logistics Meeting is to ensure that all plans for the technical and set side of the performance are agreeable by the committee, complex plans should be discussed with the relevant committee member suitably in advance.
  5. Minimum two weeks’ notice will be required if technical equipment needs to be hired in from TEC (or other external company). The Logistics Meeting is the final deadline to let the Technical Manager know what equipment needs to be hired in. All hiring of equipment is to be through the CTD or Technical Manager, not the director or producer.
  6. The Students' Union Health and Saftey officer may attend the last section of the meeting to be given a summary of and comment on what has been agreed upon.
